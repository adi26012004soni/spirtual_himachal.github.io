Spirtual Places


Churhces

1. Christ Church Shimla

Built in 1857, Christ Church in Shimla is one of the oldest churches in Himachal Pradesh and the second oldest church in North India, after St John’s Church in Meerut. The Neo-Gothic architecture, stained glass windows, and angular roof make it a sign of admiration while its prominent location right on the ridge makes it one of the most important landmarks in Shimla. If you are holidaying in Shimla, you probably can’t miss the beauty, charm and tranquil ambiance of this grand structure. 

Location: Ridge To US Club, The Mall, Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
          Opening hours: 8 am to 6 pm

2. Christ Church Kasauli

Kasauli is a quaint holiday abode between Shimla and Kalka and Christ Church is one of the tempting attractions here. Located at the heart of this town, Christ Church is an iconic building in Kasauli. This is an Anglican church built in 1853 and the Gothic style architecture, peaceful environment, and surrounding lush green scenery not only makes it one of the most visited churches in Himachal Pradesh but also makes it one of the relaxing places to explore. 

Location: Mall Rd, Kasauli, Himachal Pradesh
          Opening hours: 7 am to 7 pm

3. St. John’s Church Dalhousie

Surrounded by luxuriant pine forests and sprawling valleys, St. John’s Church is the first church built in Dalhousie and one of the popular Himachal Pradesh churches. The mighty stone structure depicts the typical colonial architecture and the church resembles the Roman Catholic Church of England. This is one of the primary tourist attractions in Dalhousie and people visit to admire the amazing structure and to enjoy the overwhelming serenity all around. 

Location: Gandhi Chowk, Moti Tiba, Dalhousie, Himachal Pradesh
          Opening hours: 7 am to 7 pm

Gurduwaras

1. Gurduwara Paonta Sahib

Gurudwara Paonta Sahib, is a noted Gurudwara in Paonta Sahib, District of Sirmour, Himachal Pradesh. This Gurdwara was built in memory of Guru Gobind Singh ji, the tenth guru of sikhs.The Dasam Granth was written here by Guru Gobind Singh Ji. Hence, the Gurdwara enjoys a high historic and religious importance among the followers of the Sikh religion world over.The Gurudwara have a palanquin “Palki” made of pure gold, donated by devotees.

2. Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib

Located at a distance of 4 km from Kasol along the banks of river Parvati in the Kullu District of Himachal Pradesh, Manikaran is known as a famous pilgrimage destination for both Sikhs and Hindus. A good number of temples, Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib and hot springs attract pilgrims and tourists alike. There are three hot springs where one can take a bath, one being inside the Gurudwara itself and the other two being privatised by the guesthouses. Different sections for men and women are made at the bathing place. The water in these springs contains sulphur which is capable of curing diseases. The water is hot enough to prepare food by directly placing the vessels and is served as langar.

3. Gurudwara Rewalsar Sahib

Located on the periphery of Lake Rewalsar, Gurudwara Rewalsar is an important place for pilgrimage. It is a point of great attraction for the Sikh tourists because it is said that a visit to this sacred Gurudwara brings peace and sanctity to their lives. Millions across the globe attend the fairs and festivals held in the Rewalsar Gurudwara. The Sikhs take the pain of traveling from another corner of the world to Rewalsar only to obtain the blessings of Vahiguru, their sacred Lord. During festive occasions, the Gurudwara becomes the venue of fairs, and people from across the country collectively celebrate the spirit of festivity over here. Gurudwara Rewalsar is one of the most important religious places to visit in Himachal Pradesh.
